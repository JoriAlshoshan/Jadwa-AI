1) Summary
This service project in Riyadh is feasible with a high likelihood of success and a recommendation to pursue organized execution. Focus on staged rollout, measured client acquisition, and tight cash-flow control to realize the predicted outcome.

2) Strengths
- Strong predicted success probability and alignment with Riyadh's high economic activity.
- Adequate budget (SAR 1,200,000) and a local team of 8 Saudi employees supporting Saudization and relationship-building.
- Target list of 85 establishments provides a clear initial market to pilot and scale services.

3) Risks
- Competition and client inertia in Riyadh could slow sales conversion, especially with established providers.
- Regulatory and contracting delays (licenses, commercial terms) could compress the 180-day schedule.
- Cash-flow pressure if onboarding costs and service delivery ramp faster than revenue from early clients.

4) Action Plan
- Finalize legal structure, commercial registration, and any sector-specific permits within the first 30 days.
- Define the service offering clearly, standardize deliverables, and prepare templates for contracts and SLAs.
- Segment the 85 target establishments into pilot (10–15), near-term (30–40), and longer-term groups for staged outreach.
- Launch a pilot with 10–15 establishments to validate pricing, delivery time, and feedback loops in the first 60 days.
- Implement a simple CRM and sales pipeline to track outreach, proposals, wins, and churn metrics.
- Train the 8 Saudi employees on service delivery standards, client communication, and upsell opportunities.
- Allocate marketing and B2B outreach (chambers of commerce, industry associations, LinkedIn) focused on decision-makers in Riyadh.
- Monitor weekly KPIs (pipeline, conversion rate, average deal size, cash burn) and adjust execution monthly.

- Allocate budget across staff compensation, pilot operations, sales/marketing, and a 10–15% contingency to manage delays.
- Price services to cover direct costs and include scalable packages for SMEs and larger establishments; offer pilot discounts with clear renewal terms.
- Prioritize relationship sales and partnerships with local business groups in Riyadh to accelerate trust and referrals.
